About

My passion for creation, language, and art led me to software development.

I grew up with artists in the family, many of them immigrants and refugees from faraway places with rich cultures and languages. In my childhood and adolescence, I practiced in art, ballet, and flute. In college, I studied education and linguistics of human languages at Penn State University and Université Paul Valéry, focusing on French. French led me to a fruitful career in K-12 public education as a World Languages teacher while continuing my studies of languages via ESL at Arizona State University.

I am quietly confident and perpetually curious. With a natural fascination for learning, I like to define myself based on what I set out to do rather than what I have already accomplished. My goal is to collaborate with talented people to create digital products for both business and consumer use. While I continue to study software development, my U. Penn coursework in coding comes in three phases: foundation, technique, and performance.
